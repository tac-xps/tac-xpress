# Tac-Xpress: Customer Acquisition Playbook

## The 10-Minute Demo Script

### Minute 0-1: The Hook
"Show me your current tracking spreadsheet."
*[They open Excel with 200 rows, 15 columns, 3 tabs]*
"Now watch this."

### Minute 1-3: The Dashboard
- 5 active shipments with real ports (Shanghai → LA).
- Mapbox satellite view with pulsing current location marker.
- Stats: 3 in transit, 1 at customs, 1 delivered.

### Minute 3-5: The Security Moment
- Open an Incognito window, log in as "warehouse worker".
- Same dashboard, but "Edit" buttons are gone, replaced by Capability boundaries.
"This isn't CSS hiding. This is cryptographic capability tokens. Your warehouse worker literally cannot see data they're not authorized for."

### Minute 5-7: The Audit Trail
- Click shipment → show tracking timeline with SHA-256 hashes.
"If customs disputes your delivery time, this hash chain is court-admissible evidence. It is an immutable, cryptographic ledger."

### Minute 7-9: The Warehouse View
- Tablet mode → `/dashboard/warehouse`.
- Large touch targets, scan buttons, filtered statuses.
"Your dock worker taps 'Scan In', sees exactly what they need."

### Minute 9-10: The Close
"Track 5 containers through Tac-Xpress for 30 days. If your team doesn't demand to keep it, you owe us nothing."

## Pricing Strategy

| Tier | Price | What They Get |
|------|-------|---------------|
| **Freemium** | $0 | 10 shipments/month, 3 users, 1 carrier |
| **Growth** | $299/mo | Unlimited everything, all carriers, API read access |
| **Enterprise** | $1,499/mo | Ghost Fleet simulation, custom RLS, white-label, compliance exports |

**Why this works:** Freemium gets them tracking their first container with zero friction. Growth is cheaper than their current labor cost ($500+/month in manual tracking). Enterprise sells the moats.
